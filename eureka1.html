During my 4 years on the team, I have mainly acted as a structures and test engineer. My overall main contributions include the design, manufacturing, and assembly of our cart testing system and flight system, and the development and enactment of our testing procedures and campaign. Smaller general tasks would include helping keep CAD designs updated; documenting design decisions and miscellaneous procedural tasks, and performing data analysis on test results.

The current goal of the team is to develop a liquid propane and liquid oxygen (LOx) pressurant fed rocket with a target altitude of 35,000 ft and a speed of Mach 2.2. The team has achieved 10 successful static fires, including 2 with a vertical flight system, that produced a max of 2.6 kN of thrust in the journey to achieve our goal.

In recent months, my main responsibilities on the team have included the design and enactment of test procedures, and the setting of performance criteria in order to determine a comprehensive testing campaign. Due to testing requiring the use of cryogenic fluids and high pressure gases, a significant part of our testing procedures is designed to ensure safe handling of propellants and gases, and that sufficient data is gathered to know the physical state of our system. On a higher level, our testing campaign is then designed to ensure that we gather sufficient data and results to fully characterize our system and have full confidence that desired performance targets can be met. The general structure for testing procedures and campaigns is:

  • Single Test Procedure
  • Ground Station Setup
  • System Setup and Checkouts
  • Pressurant Fill
  • Propellant Fill
  • Sartup Procedures
  • System Securing
  • General Testing Campaign
  • Development Testing
  • Qualification Testing
  • Acceptance Testing

My main contributions when our propulsion system was being designed were finalizing design for our LOx center pintle injector, and design and production of our main valve assembly.

For the pintle injector, I established the GD&T paramteters for all subparts: the top plate, bottom plate, and pintle. I also participated in design reviews with Astra Space Inc. to validate design parameters and manufactureability as the actual production of our injectors would be outsourced to them.

In designing our main valve assembly, I designed our method of actuating our propellant feed ball valves, managed the routing of all propellant lines including feed lines, fill lines, and vent lines. A CAD of the final assembly is shown below.
