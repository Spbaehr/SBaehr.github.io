LAD-4 was the first major project that I worked on in SEB. My major contributions included the design and manufacturing of the custom composite airframe, the manufacturing of the modular fin can used to fixture the fins, and assembly of the commercial soiid rocket motor.

For making the airframe, I helped manufacture the two tubes that would comprise the main rocket body and a nose cone. The parts were composed of a fiberglass and epoxy composite manufactured using the hand-layup method. A mandrel was used for the shaping of the tubes while a negative mold was used for the nose cone. A final layer of carbon fibver and epoxy was then aided for an aesthetic appeal. The separate sections were then connected using a phenolic tubing of slightly smaller diamter and Rivnuts.


In-flight video of launch (2x speed)

The fin can for the rocket was deisgned to be modular allowing for easy installement and removal of indiviual fins. A through-wall mounting system was designed for this purpose. It included centering rings attached to the motor housing that would attach to the roots of the fins as well. Slots were then cut into the aft section of the rocket that mated with slots cut into the leading edge of the fins. Lastly, L-brackets were attached to the back edge of the fins and the aft airframer section to prevent fin oscillation and ensure alignment.
