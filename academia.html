Publications

Citation:

Chen, A., Baehr, S., Turner, A., Zhang, Z. Gu, G. (2021). Carbon Fiber Reinforced Polymer Composites: A Comparison of Manufacturing Methods on Mechanical Properties. International Journal of Lightweight Materials and Manufacture, 4(4), 468-479. https://doi.org/10.1016/j.ijlmm.2021.04.001

Description:

This was an independent research project conducted by two other undergraduates and me with mentoring from a graduate student and a professor. The aim was to compare laminar composites manufactured via additive manufacturing to those produced under traditional methods, in terms of mechanical properties such as ultimate tensile strength and flexural modulus. Laminar specimens were produced in a variety of methods, then subjected to testing under ASTM standards. The purpose was to use the comparison between the mechanical properties of composite laminae produced in these various methods to provide perspective on the viability of novel additive manufacturing methods for large-scale production of composites.
